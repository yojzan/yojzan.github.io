How the Air Remembers

Somewhere, a wind learned your shape,
and the clouds held themselves still to keep it.
The air remembers how you pass through it,
though you walk freely through streets that feel awake.

A leaf once fell on you; it still lies there, unable to move.
The trees keep breathing, slow and patient,
hoping and dreaming in place,
though you return to them often.

The flowers that grow back at me
have never learned your name,
yet their open faces
speak of you a thousand times.
