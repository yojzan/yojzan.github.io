gravity

You are not the reason I breathe.
You are what breathing became
once it understood itself.

Before you, I moved through rooms
the way light moves through empty houses
present, but not witnessed.

I have loved you in the only way
a river knows the sea -
not by arriving,
but by never once considering elsewhere.

Your hands do not hold me.
They remind me what solidity is,
the way ground reminds the foot
it was never really falling.

I was complete before you,
the way a door is complete
before it learns there is an outside.
